Introduction to Geography

Geography is the study of Earth's landscapes, environments, and human interactions with the natural world. It combines physical features like mountains, rivers, and climate with human aspects such as population, culture, and urbanization.

Key Concepts

  • Physical Geography: Landforms, rivers, oceans, and climate zones.
  • Human Geography: Population, cities, languages, and cultural patterns.
  • Environmental Geography: Conservation, sustainability, and resource management.

Fun Geography Facts

Mount Everest is the tallest mountain above sea level at 8,848 meters. The Amazon Rainforest produces 20% of the world's oxygen. Geography helps us understand global interconnections, environmental challenges, and cultural diversity.
